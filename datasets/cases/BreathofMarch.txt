Title: "Breath of Mars"

shot1: (Act 1 - Setup) Inside the Mars habitat dome at sunrise, Dr. Hana Yu — mid-30s, focused, calm in her red-environment suit — powers on her portable atmosphere sampler and says, half to herself and half to the device, “If you and I can prove the air’s getting safer, he can actually live here with me.”
shot2: (Act 1 - Setup) Later that morning, out on the red plains near the rover, Hana kneels in the dust and plants the sampler in the soil, letting it draw in air and hum softly while she imagines telling her partner, “Yes, you can come; it’s breathable enough for long-term work.”
shot3: (Act 2 - Rising Problem) Hours later on a rocky canyon ridge, the sampler’s light flickers yellow instead of green, and Hana frowns, realizing the readings are unstable and not yet safe for permanent settlement.
shot4: (Act 2 - Commitment) She picks up the sampler, holds it close to her chest, and says quietly into her suit mic, “I’m not sending the message home yet — not until I’m sure,” and starts walking toward an older outpost farther from base.
shot5: (Act 2 - Complication) At the abandoned comms tower site, with wind sweeping red dust around her helmet, she resets the sampler’s intake manually and runs another test, telling it, “Come on, tell me we’re close; tell me we’re almost there.”
shot6: (Act 2 - Turning Point) The sampler chimes and finally glows steady green, and Hana laughs out loud in the empty Martian silence, realizing the air mix is stabilizing faster than forecast and that it’s not just survivable — it’s becoming livable.
shot7: (Act 3 - Resolution Begins) Back inside the habitat dome at evening, Hana sets the sampler on the dining table like a trophy, records her official report, and says, smiling, “Surface oxygen viability within safe range for civilian arrival — recommendation: approved.”
shot8: (Act 3 - Resolution / Promise) As the sky outside shifts to deep rust and violet, Hana sits alone with the sampler, resting her hands on it, and whispers with quiet relief, “Pack your bags; Mars is ready for you now.”
