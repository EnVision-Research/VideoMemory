Title: "Signal on Europa"

shot1: On the frozen surface of Europa, Dr. Mira Chen — 34 years old, calm eyes, dark hair pulled into a tight low braid, wearing a white-and-gray pressurized exploration suit — holds a glowing beacon transmitter in both hands and says softly, “Okay, let’s see if you’re really listening to me today.”
shot2: She plants the beacon transmitter upright in the ice beside her boots and watches it pulse a soft blue light into the mist, sending her message into the subsurface ocean below.
shot3: She leans in close to the beacon and records: “Day 42. If anyone — or anything — can hear me down there, I just want to talk,” her voice careful and kind.
shot4: The beacon flickers and hums back at her in a repeating rhythm, and she kneels beside it, smiling in surprise like she just heard someone say hello.
shot5: She adjusts the beacon’s frequency dials with gloved fingers and says, “I’m going to match your pattern, okay?” and taps out a slow reply signal like teaching a new friend a word.
shot6: The ice around her boots gives a gentle, answering vibration, and she laughs into her helmet mic, telling the beacon, “See? I knew you weren’t just picking up static.”
shot7: She cradles the beacon transmitter against her suit and says quietly, “If there’s life down there, I want them to know we arrived gentle.”
shot8: As Jupiter rises huge and orange on the horizon, Mira tucks the beacon under her arm and walks back toward base, already planning tomorrow’s conversation.
