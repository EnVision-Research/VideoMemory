Title: Cyan Portfolio

Shot1: In a vast, echoing archive library, a researcher named FINN (20s, with intense focus, wearing a simple t-shirt and jeans) carefully removes a huge, ancient map from a large, bright-cyan portfolio case (a modern, plastic artist's portfolio, its shockingly bright blue color an anachronism among the old shelves).

Shot2: The portfolio case lies open on a massive oak table. The map inside is old and brittle, showing coastlines and islands that are unfamiliar.

Shot3: Finn is in a small boat, motoring across a choppy sea. He consults a laminated copy of the ancient map, comparing it to the GPS on his phone.

Shot4: He arrives at a small, uninhabited island that is not on any modern charts. It is ringed by sharp, volcanic rocks.

Shot5: He beaches the boat and begins to explore the island, pushing his way through thick, tropical foliage.

Shot6: He finds the ruins of a stone structure, almost completely consumed by the jungle. It matches a symbol on his map.

Shot7: He searches the ruins, but finds nothing of significance. He feels a growing sense of disappointment, as if he has followed a ghost story.

Shot8: As he sits to rest, he notices the pattern of the tides. The water is receding much further than usual, exposing a sandbar that connects to a smaller, nearby sea stack.

Shot9: He realizes the map wasn't pointing to the main island, but to this temporary, tide-dependent location. He races across the sandbar before the tide turns.

Shot10: In a small cave at the base of the sea stack, he finds what he was looking for: a small, weathered wooden chest half-buried in the sand.

Shot11: He does not open the chest. His goal was only to verify its existence. He takes a photo for proof, a look of academic triumph on his face.

Shot12: Back in the quiet archive library, Finn carefully places his photographs and notes into the bright-cyan portfolio alongside the ancient map. He closes it, having confirmed a forgotten piece of history.
