Title: Crimson Garment Bag

Shot1: On a polished dance studio floor, an older dancer, ISABELLA (70s, poised and graceful, with her hair in a neat bun) holds a large, crimson garment bag (made of a rich, velvety material, its deep red color suggesting elegance and importance).

Shot2: The crimson garment bag hangs from a hook on the back of a door in a theater's dressing room. The room is empty, lit only by the classic ring of bulbs around the mirror.

Shot3: Isabella stands in the wings of a grand, empty theater, looking out at the dark expanse of seats. She breathes in the unique smell of the stage.

Shot4: She walks to the center of the stage, her soft shoes making no sound. The ghost light provides the only illumination.

Shot5: She begins to dance. There is no music. Her movements are slow at first, filled with memory, tracing the steps of a famous role from her youth.

Shot6: As she dances, her movements become stronger and more confident. The years seem to fall away, and the muscle memory of a lifetime of performance takes over.

Shot7: She stumbles slightly, a brief reminder of her age. She stops, catching her breath, a flicker of frustration crossing her features.

Shot8: She closes her eyes, not trying to remember the steps, but the feeling of the music and the emotion of the character she once played.

Shot9: She begins to move again, this time with less technical precision but with far more emotional depth. It is a beautiful, deeply personal performance for an audience of ghosts.

Shot10: She finishes her dance, holding a final, perfect pose in the center of the vast, empty stage. She is not sad, but content.

Shot11: Back in the dressing room, she carefully unzips the garment bag, revealing a stunning, ornate ballet costume from decades past. She touches the fabric gently.

Shot12: Isabella leaves the theater through the stage door into the night. She holds the crimson garment bag close, having said a private, final goodbye to the stage and the dance that defined her life.
