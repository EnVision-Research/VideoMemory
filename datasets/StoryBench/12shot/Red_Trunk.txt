Title: Red Trunk

Shot1: In a dusty, forgotten attic, SAM (50s, a man with a nostalgic air, wearing old jeans and a faded t-shirt) pulls a large, bright-red metal trunk (a classic steamer trunk from a bygone era, with leather straps and a high-gloss, fire-engine red finish) from under a pile of white sheets.

Shot2: The bright-red trunk sits in the back of a pickup truck, driving through rolling green countryside.

Shot3: Sam arrives at a small, waterfront cottage that looks like it hasn't been visited in years. He uses an old key to open the front door.

Shot4: Inside, the furniture is covered in dusty sheets. Sunbeams slice through the grimy windows, illuminating dancing dust motes.

Shot5: He carries the heavy trunk into the main room and sets it down. He struggles for a moment with the rusted latches.

Shot6: He finally gets it open. The trunk is filled with old photo albums, bundles of letters tied with ribbon, and a child's worn teddy bear.

Shot7: Sam sits on the floor, looking through an old photo album. He pauses on a picture of himself as a young boy with his parents in front of this very cottage. A sad smile touches his lips.

Shot8: He reads one of the letters, written in his mother's handwriting. He is completely absorbed in the past.

Shot9: He finds a small, hand-carved wooden boat at the bottom of the trunk. He holds it in his palm, lost in a powerful memory.

Shot10: With newfound resolve, he stands up and begins pulling the sheets off the furniture, letting light and air back into the long-dormant room.

Shot11: He is now on the small wooden dock outside the cottage, sweeping away years of leaves and grime. The place is slowly coming back to life.

Shot12: As evening approaches, Sam sits on the end of the clean dock, skipping a stone across the calm water. The open bright-red trunk is on the porch of the cottage behind him, its contents representing a past he has finally reconnected with.
