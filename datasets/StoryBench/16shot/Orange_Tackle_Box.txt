Title: Orange Tackle Box

Shot1: MARK (55, rugged, wearing a fishing vest) stands on a wooden dock by a calm lake, holding his BRIGHT-ORANGE TACKLE BOX (a large, multi-tray plastic box).

Shot2: He opens the box, selects his favorite, time-tested lure, and ties it to his line.

Shot3: He casts his line into the glassy water. He waits.

Shot4: An hour passes. The water is still. Not a single bite.

Shot5: He reels in his line and casts again, to a different spot.

Shot6: He sees a splash near some reeds far across the lake.

Shot7: He gets into his small rowboat, rows to the new spot, and casts again.

Shot8: His line immediately snags on something underwater. A submerged log.

Shot9: He pulls and wiggles the line, trying to free it.

Shot10: The line snaps. His favorite lure is gone.

Shot11: Mark (looking dejected) rows back to the dock.

Shot12: He sits on the dock, ready to pack up and go home.

Shot13: He idly opens his tackle box and sees a strange, dusty lure in the bottom he's never used.

Shot14: With a sigh, he ties on the new lure and makes one last, lazy cast from the dock.

Shot15: The water explodes as something huge takes the lure. He fights the fish, his rod bent in half.

Shot16: After a struggle, he lands the biggest fish he's ever caught. He smiles, unhooks it, releases it, and closes his bright-orange tackle box.
