Title: Yellow Seedling Tray

Shot1: MR. HARRIS (65, with kind eyes and earth-stained overalls) stands in his greenhouse, holding a BRIGHT YELLOW SEEDLING TRAY (a large, rectangular plastic tray).

Shot2: He carefully places the tray, which holds one single, tiny seed, on his workbench. This is his last seed for the town's flower competition.

Shot3: He turns to grab a watering can. A strong gust of wind blows through the open greenhouse door.

Shot4: A bag of potting soil is knocked over, spilling onto the bench and flipping the seedling tray. The tray is now upside down, the seed gone.

Shot5: Mr. Harris (now looking distressed) drops the watering can and frantically brushes the soil aside.

Shot6: He gets on his hands and knees, searching the greenhouse floor, pushing aside pots and tools.

Shot7: He sees a mouse scurry from under the bench and out the open door.

Shot8: Mr. Harris follows the mouse's path out into his garden. He loses the trail in the thick grass.

Shot9: He sits heavily on a stone bench, looking defeated, his head in his hands.

Shot10: He looks down at his feet. He notices a tiny crack in one of the paving stones.

Shot11: Something is wedged in the crack. He kneels, pulling his reading glasses from his pocket.

Shot12: It's the seed. It must have stuck to the mouse and fallen off.

Shot13: Mr. Harris (a huge smile spreading across his face) gently picks up the tiny seed.

Shot14: Back in the greenhouse, he plants the seed in a secure, heavy ceramic pot.

Shot15: Weeks later, a unique, vibrant blue flower, unlike any other, blooms from the pot.

Shot16: Mr. Harris stands proudly next to his flower, holding the (now empty) bright yellow seedling tray in his other hand.
