Title: Blue Tarp

Shot1: LEO (28, with a pensive expression and paint-stained jeans) stands in his empty white studio, holding a LARGE, BRIGHT BLUE TARP (a heavy-duty plastic sheet).

Shot2: A huge, blank canvas sits on an easel in the middle of the room. He has artist's block.

Shot3: He sighs and tosses the tarp over a pile of furniture in the corner, covering it completely.

Shot4: He grabs his keys and leaves the studio, frustrated.

Shot5: Leo walks through a busy, grey city. The shapes of buildings and cars look dull and uninspiring.

Shot6: He sits on a bench in a park, but the colors of the flowers seem flat.

Shot7: Dark clouds gather. A heavy, sudden rainstorm begins.

Shot8: People run for cover. Leo gets soaked in seconds. He gives up and starts running back.

Shot9: He bursts back into his studio (now soaking wet, hair plastered to his head).

Shot10: The rain is beating hard on the studio's skylight.

Shot11: The light from the skylight shines through the rain, creating distorted, beautiful, watery patterns on the bright blue tarp.

Shot12: Leo stops, mesmerized by the shifting shapes and colors.

Shot13: A new look comes over his face. He grabs a brush and a tub of white paint.

Shot14: He begins painting furiously on the blank canvas, not painting the tarp, but the *feeling* of the light.

Shot15: The canvas is now covered in wild, abstract swirls of white, grey, and deep blue. It's full of energy.

Shot16: Leo (now covered in new paint, smiling) stands back to look at his work, his empty brush in one hand and a corner of the bright blue tarp in the other.
