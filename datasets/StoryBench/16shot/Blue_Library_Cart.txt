Title: Blue Library Cart

Shot1: ELIZA (60, with sharp eyes and wearing spectacles) pushes a BRIGHT-BLUE LIBRARY CART (a large, three-tiered metal rolling cart) through the silent history section.

Shot2: She is reshelving books. She stops at a row of very old, leather-bound volumes.

Shot3: She picks up one book, "A History of the Town," to reshelve it, and it feels too light.

Shot4: Eliza opens the book. A large section of pages from the middle has been crudely torn out.

Shot5: She (looking horrified) immediately takes the book to the front desk.

Shot6: She checks the library's digital records. There is no note of the damage.

Shot7: She checks the security logs for the "Special Collections" room, but the last checkout was weeks ago.

Shot8: Eliza walks to the Special Collections room, a small, secure room with heavy wooden tables.

Shot9: She looks on the floor, under the tables, and even in the wastebasket. Nothing.

Shot10: She is deeply upset. She believes a priceless part of the town's history is gone forever.

Shot11: She walks back to the main desk, pushing her empty cart, feeling defeated.

Shot12: A student returns a different book: "Modern Architecture."

Shot13: Eliza takes the book to reshelve. As she opens it to check the binding, something falls out.

Shot14: It's the missing, folded-up pages from the history book, used as a bookmark.

Shot15: Eliza (gasping, then looking furious, then relieved) carefully picks up the pages.

Shot16: Eliza places the damaged book and the recovered pages together on her bright-blue library cart to take to the repair archive.
