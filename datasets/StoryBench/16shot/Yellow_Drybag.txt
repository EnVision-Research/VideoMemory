Title: Yellow Drybag

Shot1: CHLOE (27, in athletic hiking gear) stands at a sunny mountain trailhead, securing a LARGE, BRIGHT YELLOW DRYBAG (a rolled-top waterproof bag) to her backpack.

Shot2: She starts up the trail, a map in her hand. The sign says "Summit: 5 Miles."

Shot3: The trail is beautiful, winding through green forests. The sky is blue.

Shot4: After two hours, the trail becomes steep and rocky, above the tree line.

Shot5: The sky darkens quickly. Grey clouds roll in over the peak.

Shot6: A cold wind picks up. It starts to drizzle.

Shot7: The drizzle turns into a heavy, cold, driving rain. There is no cover.

Shot8: Chloe (now soaked and shivering) pulls her rain jacket tighter, but she's getting wet.

Shot9: She continues hiking, her head down, just trying to reach the next trail marker.

Shot10: She sees a small, wooden sign half-hidden by a rock: "Emergency Shelter."

Shot11: She follows a small, muddy path off the main trail.

Shot12: She finds a small, three-walled wooden lean-to. She ducks inside, out of the wind.

Shot13: She's shivering badly. She unclips the drybag from her pack.

Shot14: She unrolls the top. Inside, her spare sweater, wool socks, and a small towel are perfectly, completely dry.

Shot15: Chloe (now wearing the dry sweater and socks) smiles, wrapped in her towel, and looks out at the storm.

Shot16: She sips water from her bottle, holding the life-saving, bright yellow drybag on her lap.
