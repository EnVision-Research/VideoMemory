Title: Red Jerrycan

Shot1: RENA (29, practical, in greasy overalls) stands in her messy garage, holding a BRIGHT RED, HEAVY-DUTY JERRYCAN (a 5-gallon metal fuel can).

Shot2: The can is empty. In front of her is a beautiful, classic car covered in dust, which she is trying to restore.

Shot3: She gets in the driver's seat and turns the key. The engine just makes a sad clicking sound.

Shot4: She gets out, opens the hood, and stares at the complex, dirty engine.

Shot5: Rena spends hours cleaning parts. She replaces the spark plugs.

Shot6: She gets back in the car and tries the key again. Click, click, sputter. Nothing.

Shot7: She kicks the front tire, her frustration obvious.

Shot8: She leaves the garage and goes inside her house.

Shot9: She sits at a desk and pulls out a very thick, old technical manual for the car.

Shot10: She reads for a long time, flipping through diagrams of the wiring.

Shot11: Her eyes stop on a small, annotated note about a "jumper wire" near the ignition coil.

Shot12: She runs back to the garage, holding the manual.

Shot13: She leans into the engine bay and finds a loose wire, just as the diagram showed. She reconnects it.

Shot14: She gets in the car, takes a breath, and turns the key. The engine roars to life.

Shot15: Rena cheers, jumping out of the car.

Shot16: She picks up the bright red jerrycan, a huge smile on her face, and heads out the door to get gas for the car's first drive.
