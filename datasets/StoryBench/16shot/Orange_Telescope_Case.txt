Title: Orange Telescope Case

Shot1: DR. ARIS (45, wearing a heavy parka) arrives at a dark, remote hilltop, carrying a LARGE, BRIGHT ORANGE TELESCOPE CASE (a hard-shell, foam-lined protective case).

Shot2: He sets up his expensive telescope on its tripod under a perfectly clear, starry sky.

Shot3: He unlatches the orange case to get the final, most important eyepiece.

Shot4: The custom-molded foam slot for the eyepiece is empty. He must have forgotten it.

Shot5: Dr. Aris (now looking frantic) checks his pockets. He checks the ground around the tripod.

Shot6: He gets a flashlight and retraces his steps back to his car, sweeping the light over the rocky path.

Shot7: He can't find it. The eyepiece is gone.

Shot8: He returns to his telescope, defeated. He looks up at the sky. A comet, the reason he came, is just visible.

Shot9: He sits on the cold ground, frustrated, knowing he's missing the event.

Shot10: He notices the small, secondary "finder scope" still attached to the side of the main telescope.

Shot11: He scoffs. It's just for alignment, barely more than a monocular.

Shot12: With nothing else to do, he leans down and peers through the tiny finder scope.

Shot13: The view is small and unmagnified, but the comet is there, a clear, beautiful smudge against the stars.

Shot14: He stays there for a long time, watching the comet through the tiny lens. A small, peaceful smile forms.

Shot15: The next morning, the sun rises. He begins packing his equipment.

Shot16: Dr. Aris closes the lid of the bright orange telescope case, a look of contentment on his face, and heads home.
