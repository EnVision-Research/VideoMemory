Title: Green Umbrella

Shot1: SAM (38, with tired eyes, wearing a bathrobe in the middle of the day) stares at a blank laptop screen. In the corner stands a LARGE, NEON-GREEN UMBRELLA (a "golf" sized umbrella).

Shot2: He's a writer with writer's block. He types a sentence: "The sun was hot."

Shot3: He deletes the sentence. He gets up and paces his apartment.

Shot4: He looks out the window. It's a perfectly beautiful, sunny day. He scowls.

Shot5: He decides to go for a walk, hoping for inspiration. He leaves the apartment, ignoring the umbrella.

Shot6: Sam is in a city park, sitting on a bench. He's surrounded by happy people. He feels worse.

Shot7: A dark cloud appears from nowhere and quickly covers the sun.

Shot8: A sudden, torrential downpour begins. People scream and scatter.

Shot9: Sam is caught in the open, instantly soaked. He runs for cover under a small tree, but it's useless.

Shot10: He is completely, miserably wet.

Shot11: He thinks about the huge, dry umbrella sitting in his apartment. He starts to laugh at the irony.

Shot12: An idea forms. He starts laughing harder.

Shot13: He runs all the way back to his apartment, dripping water everywhere.

Shot14: He (now soaking wet, but with a manic energy) sits at his laptop and starts typing furiously.

Shot15: He writes: "Of all the things Frank hated, it was the sun. But the rain... the rain was a different story."

Shot16: Sam continues to type, a smile on his face, as the large, neon-green umbrella stands forgotten in the corner.
