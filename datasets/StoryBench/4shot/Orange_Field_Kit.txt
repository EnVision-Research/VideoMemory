Title: Orange Field Kit

Shot1: On a misty, rocky shoreline, BEN (40s, a geologist in a waterproof jacket and boots) chips a small sample from a large boulder. His bright-orange field kit (a hard-shell, waterproof case, about the size of a briefcase, in a high-visibility safety orange) rests on a flat rock nearby.

Shot2: The bright-orange field kit sits on the passenger seat of a rugged 4x4 vehicle, parked at the edge of a dense, mossy forest.

Shot3: Ben is wading through a shallow, crystal-clear river, using a walking stick for balance. He carefully examines the stones on the riverbed as he goes.

Shot4: Ben has found a small, hidden cave behind a curtain of ivy. Inside, he opens his bright-orange field kit. He takes out a magnifying glass and examines a rock from the cave wall that glitters with a vein of quartz. He has found what he was looking for.
