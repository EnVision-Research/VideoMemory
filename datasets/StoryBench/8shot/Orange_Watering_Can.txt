Title: Orange Watering Can

Shot1: MAYA (25, wearing denim overalls and a sun hat) stands on a grey, concrete rooftop, holding a large, BRIGHT ORANGE WATERING CAN (made of shiny plastic).

Shot2: She kneels by a small wooden planter, pouring soil from a bag. A strong gust of wind blows some of the soil away.

Shot3: Maya carefully plants a few seeds, but a pigeon lands nearby and immediately pecks at the spot. She shoos it away, looking discouraged.

Shot4: She looks around the empty, windy rooftop, her shoulders slumped. The city noise seems very loud.

Shot5: Maya is now on her small, sheltered apartment balcony, one floor below the roof. It is quiet and protected from the wind.

Shot6: She moves a single ceramic pot into a patch of sunlight on the balcony floor and carefully plants her remaining seeds in it.

Shot7: Days later, a tiny green sprout is pushing up from the soil in the pot.

Shot8: Maya (smiling) gently pours water from the bright orange watering can onto the new sprout.
