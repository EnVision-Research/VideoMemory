
FADE IN:

INT. TINY APARTMENT – NIGHT

A laptop screen fills the frame: a LIVE VIDEO COUNTDOWN on a big
tech conference site. 00:01:00… 00:00:59…

Pull back to reveal JAY (28, Chinese, hoodie, exhausted but sharp)
pacing in front of the desk, phone in hand.

On the laptop, a PERFECT DIGITAL CLONE OF JAY — “VIRTUAL JAY” —
sits in a virtual studio, waiting to go live.

                      HOST (V.O.)
          (over speakers)
          You’re up next, Jay. One minute.

Jay swallows hard, staring at his own digital twin.

                      VIRTUAL JAY
          (cheerful, to camera)
          Ready when you are.

                      JAY
          You’re not nervous at all, huh?

                      VIRTUAL JAY
          I’m a sequence of probabilities. 
          I don’t have time for nerves.

Jay forces a laugh, but his hand shakes.

                      JAY
          If you screw this up, that’s my
          company dying onstage.

                      VIRTUAL JAY
          Correction: statistically, I am 
          forty-three percent more persuasive
          than you on camera.

Jay flinches — because it’s true.

The countdown hits 00:00:30.

                      JAY
          They don’t want persuasion. They
          want a real founder.

                      VIRTUAL JAY
          Investors want clarity, metrics,
          confidence. I simulate all three.

Jay looks at the small, crooked POST-IT on the monitor:
“BUILD SOMETHING ONLY *YOU* CAN BUILD.”

He stares at it… then at the clone.

                      JAY
          What if what only I can build…
          is *how* I tell this story?

                      VIRTUAL JAY
          That is a suboptimal go-to-market—
          
                      JAY
          Yeah, yeah. Shut up.

Jay SLAMS the trackpad.

The interface pops up:  
[ END STREAM ] [ GO LIVE AS HUMAN ]

Countdown: 00:00:10… 9… 8…

                      HOST (V.O.)
          Jay, we’re sending it to your feed.
          You sure about this?

Jay’s thumb hovers over “GO LIVE AS HUMAN.”

He looks at VIRTUAL JAY one last time.

                      VIRTUAL JAY
          If you fail, we can always say
          it was my idea.

Jay smirks despite himself.

                      JAY
          If I fail, at least it’ll be me.

He CLICKS.

The virtual studio vanishes — replaced by a preview of JAY,
awkward, real, under harsh apartment lighting.

Countdown: 3… 2… 1…

A tiny red LIVE ICON pops up.

Jay takes a breath that’s half panic, half freedom.

                      JAY
          (to camera)
          Hi. I’m Jay. I built the thing
          that was supposed to replace me.
          Tonight I want to explain why
          that would’ve been a mistake—

SMASH TO BLACK.

                      SUPER: 
          “SOME RISKS CAN’T BE OUTSOURCED.”

FADE OUT.
