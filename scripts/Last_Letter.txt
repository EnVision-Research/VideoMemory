**TITLE: "The Last Letter"**

---

**ACT 1**

**1. EXT. PARK – DAY**

A gentle breeze rustles the trees. LUCY (28), an elegant woman in a long coat, sits on a park bench, holding a weathered envelope.

**LUCY**
(softly, to herself)
I never thought it would come to this.

She hesitates, then opens the envelope, revealing a letter inside.

**2. EXT. STREET – DAY**

A close-up of a MAN'S hand (late 20s) taking the letter from LUCY’s hand. It's JAMES (30), her ex-lover. His face is filled with regret.

**JAMES**
(softly)
You don’t have to do this. Please, Lucy.

**3. EXT. PARK – CONTINUOUS**

LUCY steps back, keeping her distance, holding the letter tightly in her hand.

**LUCY**
(voice shaking)
You made your choice, James. This is mine.

She looks at the letter, trembling as the wind blows through the trees.

---

**ACT 2**

**4. INT. COFFEE SHOP – DAY**

LUCY sits at a corner table, the letter now placed neatly before her. She stares at it, lost in thought.

**5. EXT. STREET – DAY**

JAMES walks by the coffee shop, his eyes catching the reflection of LUCY through the window. He stops and watches her for a moment.

---

**ACT 3**

**6. INT. COFFEE SHOP – DAY**

The door chimes as JAMES enters. He stands before LUCY, who looks up at him with resolve.

**JAMES**
I can’t live without you. Please... take me back.

**LUCY**
(quietly)
It’s too late, James. I’ve already made my decision.

**7. EXT. COFFEE SHOP – DAY**

LUCY walks out, the letter still clutched in her hand. As she passes by, a soft breeze picks up, and the letter flutters slightly.

**8. EXT. PARK – SUNSET**

LUCY walks toward a small bridge, the letter now crumpled in her hand. She stops, takes one last look at the sky, and releases the letter into the wind. The camera follows the letter as it floats away.

---

**FADE OUT.**
